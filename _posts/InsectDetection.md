
**Overview**: In nature, insect disaster is one of the big problems affecting crop yield. Detecting insect pests is not easy for famers, and it takes times to figure out when and where we can use protection method to protect out product. However, it is very important to provide critical information for famers to prevent the effects of insects and improve the product quality. Often, these scientific guidelines are ignored leading to famers not capturing the representative condition of the field. In this project, we propose a novel method that enables the automatic detection of insects. This is done by developing a deep learning model trained on the IP102-Dataset and outputs the possible insects in the field. The dataset contains more than 75000 images of 102 categories.
**Plan**: First, the dataset is taken from IP102-Dataset on Kaggle and modified to match with the deep learning model. Second, a detection deep learning model is developed for training the dataset. The input of the model are images from the dataset, and the output are a lists of object categories in the image along with bounding boxes in dictating position of each category.
